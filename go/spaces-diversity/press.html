León, first capital of Spain that will give a name to a street in homage to the the gays.

León will be the first Spanish capital in having a street that with the name of "June 28", in allusion to the date of celebration of the Day of the Gay Pride, will surrender homage to the gay, lesbian, bisexual and transsexual community.

The municipal group of socialists announced this way it today in a press note, after the proposal that it presented in this sense they received the seen good of the Commission of Interior of the City council of León.

NOTICE: The municipal decision was "paralyzed" by the PP once it was made with the governorship.

Diario de León, March 11 2005

Mexico: Badge in reject of homophobic action

Without being specifically a Square of the sexual diversity, in the park that is to the outskirts of the Hidalgo Underground, in the Historical Center of the Mexico City, it was placed in 2001 a badge in memory and reject to the porfirist government's repressive act toward 41 homosexual men who were surprised in a party deprived by the police of those years and imprisoned.

NotieSe, February 10, 2005

Against the discrimination
They inaugurate in Uruguay first "gay square" of Latin America

It is the first one in Latin America, and the fourth in the world, thought as an encounter place for the sexual minorities. A monument was also inaugurated "against the discrimination, the preventive wars or selective" murders, the intendant from Montevideo declared.

Montevideo became the first Ibero-American capital and the fourth in the world after Amsterdam in Holland, and New York and San Francisco, in United States in having a "Square of the Sexual Diversity".

In the place a triangular monolith of granite was erected, with rosy and black veins, with the inscription: To "honor the diversity is to honor the life: Montevideo for the respect to all gender, identity and sexual orientation".

On the monument, the activist of Diversity Uruguay, Fernando Frontán, explained to BBC World that a triangle is "what the Nazi put to the homosexuals. The same as to the Jews they were put a star, to the homosexuals a rosy triangle and to the lesbians and prostitutes one black" [...].

Diverse unanimity

The initiative was impelled by several activist groups, proposal to the intendant from Montevideo, architect Mariano Arana, and it had the support unanimously of the departmental legislative of the Uruguayan capital [...].

Diana Mines, of the Executive Committee of International Amnesty of Uruguay, she said that "if today the recovery of the rights of gay people, lesbians or travestis, intersexuals, bisexual, constitutes a flag it is because that natural right is not recognized by the group of the society" [...].

The splendid monument is in a recycled space public in full historical area of Montevideo.

BBC World News, February 2 2005
